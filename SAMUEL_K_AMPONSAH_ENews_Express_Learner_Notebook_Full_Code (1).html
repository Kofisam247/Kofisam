Project Business Statistics: E-news Express¶

Define Problem Statement and Objectives¶

Problem Statement¶

  1. Decline in monthly subscription of E-news Express as compared to the past.
  2. Current web is not well designed in terms of its outline and contents.

Objectives¶

Increase E-news Express subscribers thus testing the effectiveness of a newly created landing page by,

  1. Finding out whether users spend more time on the newly created landing page as compared to the old landing page.
  2. Establishing whether conversion depends on languge preference.
  3. Determining whether time spent on the new page is different for different users.

Data Dictionary¶

The data contains information regarding the interaction of users in both groups with the two versions of the landing page.

  1. user_id - Unique user ID of the person visiting the website

  2. group - Whether the user belongs to the first group (control) or the second group (treatment)

  3. landing_page - Whether the landing page is new or old

  4. time_spent_on_the_page - Time (in minutes) spent by the user on the landing page

  5. converted - Whether the user gets converted to a subscriber of the news portal or not

  6. language_preferred - Language chosen by the user to view the landing page

Step 1: Define the null and alternate hypotheses¶

Let $\mu_1, \mu_2$ be the mean time spent on the old and new pages respectively.

Null hypothesis

$H_0:\mu_1=\mu_2$

Against the Alternate hypothesis

$H_a:\mu_1<\mu_2$

Step 2: Select Appropriate test¶

  • Data is continuous since it is measured on a continuous scale(time).
  • Normally distributed populations from the Histogram above.
  • Independent populations - control group and treatment group.
  • Unknown standard deviations.
  • Random sampling from the population - 100 users randomly selected

Two indepedent sample t-test for means with unequal standard deviations is the appropriate test to use since it satisfies the above

Step 3: Decide the significance level¶

Significance level is given as 0.05 ($\alpha = 0.05$)

Null and alternate hypotheses¶

Let $\mu_1, \mu_2$ be the proportions of conversion on the old and new pages respectively.

Null hypothesis

$H_0:\mu_1=\mu_2$

Against the Alternate hypothesis

$H_a:\mu_1<\mu_2$

Appropriate test selection¶

  • Population is binomally distributed - User either converts or does not.
  • Population is randomly sampled as informed.
  • Can the binomial distribution approximated to normal distribution - Yes. For binary data, CLT works slower than usual. The standard thing is to check whether np and n(1-p) are greater than or equal to 10. Here, n and p refer to the sample size and sample proportion respectively.

    $np_1 = 100\cdot \frac{50}{100} =50 \geq 10\\ n(1-p_1) = 100 \cdot \frac{100-50}{100} =50 \geq 10 \\ np_2 = 100\cdot \frac{50}{100} =50 \geq 10\\ n(1-p_2) = 100 \cdot \frac{100-50}{100} =50 \geq 10 $

Observation:

Two proportion z - test is the appropriate test selection since it satisfies the above conditions

Significance level is given as 0.05 ($\alpha = 0.05$)

Let $\mu_1, \mu_2, \mu_3$ be the means of English, French and Spanish users time respectively on the new page.

Null hypothesis

$H_0: \mu_1 = \mu_2 = \mu_3$

Alternative hypothesis

$H_a: $ At least one language mean time is different from the rest.

Appropriate test: There are three population means to compare. Therefore, one way ANOVA test is the appropriate test to use. However, we need to verify the normality and equality of variance to conclude on the ANOVA test. This is done by

  1. Shapiro-Wilk's test for normality
  2. levene's test for equality of variance

Shapiro-Wilk's test

Null hypothesis

$H_0:$ Time spent on the new page follows a normal distribution against

Alternative hypothesis

$H_a:$ Time spent on the new page does not follow a normal distribution

In [58]:
# required function import
from scipy import stats

# compute p value
stat, p_value = stats.shapiro(data_new['time_spent_on_the_page'])
print("P-value:", p_value)

P-value: 0.8040040364746845

We fail to reject the null hypothesis which states that the time spent on the new page follows a normal distribution since the p value is large

Levene's test

Null hypothesis

$H_0$: All the population variances are equal

Alternative hypothesis

$H_a$: At least one variance is different from the rest

In [59]:
# required function import
from scipy.stats import levene
In [60]:
 # compute p value
stat, p_value = levene(data_new[data_new['language_preferred'] == 'English']['time_spent_on_the_page'],
                        data_new[data_new['language_preferred'] == 'French']['time_spent_on_the_page'],data_new[data_new['language_preferred'] == 'Spanish']['time_spent_on_the_page'])
print("P-value:", p_value)

P-value: 0.46711357711340173

We fail to reject the null hypothesis of equal population variances since the p value is larger than the significance level of 0.05

One way ANOVA test is therefore,the appropriate since it satisfies the list below

  • Normal distributed population
  • Equal population variances
  • Independent random samples

Significance level is given as 0.05 ($\alpha = 0.05$)

Conclusion and Business Recommendations¶

From the sample data, we can conclude that;

  • A well designed page with recommended contents will not necessarily keep users long enough on the page.
  • Language preference and conversion are related.
  • Users of different language preference spent averagely the same amount of time on the new page.
  • Conversion rate on the new page was higher as compared to the old page.

Recommendations;

  • E-news Express should consider segmenting its news items.
  • Subscription should be based on contents. (sports, movies,politics, tech, etc).
  • E-news should offer special discounts on subscription based on the time spent on their landing page.
  • Explore more language options rather than English, French and Spanish.
